On March 13, 1997, the Phoenix Lights (sometimes referred to as the "Lights Over Phoenix") were a series of widely-sighted unidentified flying objects observed in the sky over Arizona and Nevada, and the Mexican state of Sonora. Thousands of people between 7:30 pm and 10:30 pm MST described lights floating in the sky. There were two distinct events witnesses, a triangular formation of lights and a series of stationary lights seen in the Phoenix area. The US Air Force identified the second group of lights as flares dispersed from an A-10 Warthog aircraft that were on training exercises at the Barry Goldwater Range in Arizona. Witnesses claim to have observed a huge carpenter's-square-shaped UFO, containing five spherical lights. Fife Symington, the governor of Arizona at the time, said he witnessed this incident, describing the object as "otherworldly." The lights were reported to have reappeared in 2007 and 2008, and were attributed to military flares dropped by fighter aircraft at Luke Air Force Base and flares attached to helium balloons released by a civilian.
